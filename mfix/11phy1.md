### Dimensional Analysis of a Squared-Velocity Term (ID 73)

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{The dimensions of LHS are}\qquad
\left[M\right]\left[LT^{-1}\right]^2
&=\left[M\right]\left[L^2T^{-2}\right] \\
&=\left[ML^2T^{-2}\right].
\end{aligned}
$$

## Chapter 3 – Motion In A Plane

### Velocity Vector in Component Form (ID 152)

$$
\mathbf{v}
=
v_x\,\hat{\mathbf{i}}
+
v_y\,\hat{\mathbf{j}}
$$

### Velocity Components as Time Derivatives (ID 153)

$$
v_x=\frac{dx}{dt},
\qquad
v_y=\frac{dy}{dt}
$$

## Chapter 5 – Work, Energy, and Power

### Conservation of Mechanical Energy Derivation (ID 205)

$$
\begin{aligned}
(F_c+F_{nc})\,\Delta x &= \Delta K,\\
F_c\,\Delta x &= -\Delta V,\\
\Delta(K+V) &= F_{nc}\,\Delta x,\\
\Delta E &= F_{nc}\,\Delta x.
\end{aligned}
$$
