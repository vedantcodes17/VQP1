### Ionization of Acetic Acid (ID 767)

$$
\mathrm{HAc(aq)}
\rightleftharpoons
\mathrm{H^+(aq)} + \mathrm{Ac^-(aq)}
$$

#### ICE Table

|                     | HAc (M) | H⁺ (M) | Ac⁻ (M) |
|---------------------|:-------:|:------:|:-------:|
| Initial concentration | 0.05 | 0 | 0 |
| Change | $-x$ | $+x$ | $+x$ |
| Equilibrium concentration | $0.05-x$ | $x$ | $x$ |

Let $x$ be the extent of ionization of acetic acid.

The acid dissociation constant is

$$
K_a=\frac{[\mathrm{H^+}][\mathrm{Ac^-}]}{[\mathrm{HAc}]}
=\frac{x \cdot x}{0.05-x}
=\frac{x^2}{0.05-x}
$$

Since acetic acid is a weak acid,

$$
x \ll 0.05
$$

Therefore,

$$
0.05-x \approx 0.05
$$

Hence,

$$
K_a \approx \frac{x^2}{0.05}
$$

Substituting

$$
K_a = 1.8 \times 10^{-5}
$$

gives

$$
1.8 \times 10^{-5}
=
\frac{x^2}{0.05}
$$

$$
x^2
=
(1.8 \times 10^{-5})(0.05)
=
9.0 \times 10^{-7}
$$

$$
x
=
\sqrt{9.0 \times 10^{-7}}
=
9.49 \times 10^{-4}\ \mathrm{M}
$$

Finally,

$$
\mathrm{pH}
=
-\log(9.49 \times 10^{-4})
\approx 3.02
$$

### Interval Notation: [a,b], [a,b), (a,b] (ID 186)

The interval which contains the end points also is called closed interval and is denoted by $[a,b]$. Thus

$$
[a,b]=\{x:a\le x\le b\}
$$

We can also have intervals closed at one end and open at the other, i.e.,

$$
[a,b)=\{x:a\le x<b\}
$$

is an interval from $a$ to $b$, including $a$ but excluding $b$.

$$
(a,b]=\{x:a<x\le b\}
$$

is an interval from $a$ to $b$, including $b$ but excluding $a$.

### Simplifying a Rational Expression (ID 242)

$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{x-2}{x^{2}-x}-\frac{1}{x^{3}-3x^{2}+2x}
&=\frac{x-2}{x(x-1)}-\frac{1}{x(x-1)(x-2)}\\

### Derivative of sin²x (Product Rule) (ID 443)

$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{df(x)}{dx}
&=\frac{d}{dx}(\sin x\cdot\sin x)\\
&=(\sin x)'\sin x+\sin x(\sin x)'\\
&=(\cos x)\sin x+\sin x(\cos x)\\
&=2\sin x\cos x\\
&=\sin 2x.
\end{aligned}
$$

### Addition Theorem of Probability (Sample-Space Derivation) (ID 148)

$$
\begin{aligned}
P(A)+P(B)
&=\left[\sum_{\omega_i\in A}P(\omega_i)\right]
+\left[\sum_{\omega_i\in B}P(\omega_i)\right]\\
&=\left[\sum_{\omega_i\in(A-B)}P(\omega_i)\right]
+\left[\sum_{\omega_i\in(A\cap B)}P(\omega_i)\right]\\
&\quad+\left[\sum_{\omega_i\in(B-A)}P(\omega_i)\right]
+\left[\sum_{\omega_i\in(A\cap B)}P(\omega_i)\right]\\
&=P(A\cup B)+P(A\cap B).
\end{aligned}
$$

## Chapter 7 – Binomial Theorem

### Binomial Theorem – Inductive Step P(k+1) (ID 24)

$$
P(k+1):\;
(a+b)^{k+1}
=
{}^{k+1}C_{0}a^{k+1}
+
{}^{k+1}C_{1}a^{k}b
+
{}^{k+1}C_{2}a^{k-1}b^{2}
+\cdots+
{}^{k+1}C_{k}ab^{k}
+
{}^{k+1}C_{k+1}b^{k+1}
$$

### Binomial Theorem – Proof of Inductive Step (ID 25)

$$
\begin{aligned}
(a+b)^{k+1}
&=(a+b)(a+b)^k\\
&=(a+b)\left(
{}^{k}C_{0}a^{k}
+{}^{k}C_{1}a^{k-1}b
+{}^{k}C_{2}a^{k-2}b^{2}
+\cdots
+{}^{k}C_{k}b^{k}
\right)\\
&={}^{k}C_{0}a^{k+1}
+{}^{k}C_{1}a^{k}b
+{}^{k}C_{2}a^{k-1}b^{2}
+\cdots
+{}^{k}C_{k-1}a^{2}b^{k-1}
+{}^{k}C_{k}ab^{k}\\
&\quad
+{}^{k}C_{0}a^{k}b
+{}^{k}C_{1}a^{k-1}b^{2}
+{}^{k}C_{2}a^{k-2}b^{3}
+\cdots
+{}^{k}C_{k-1}ab^{k}
+{}^{k}C_{k}b^{k+1}\\
&={}^{k}C_{0}a^{k+1}
+\left({}^{k}C_{1}+{}^{k}C_{0}\right)a^{k}b
+\left({}^{k}C_{2}+{}^{k}C_{1}\right)a^{k-1}b^{2}
+\cdots\\
&\quad
+\left({}^{k}C_{k}+{}^{k}C_{k-1}\right)ab^{k}
+{}^{k}C_{k}b^{k+1}\\
&={}^{k+1}C_{0}a^{k+1}
+{}^{k+1}C_{1}a^{k}b
+{}^{k+1}C_{2}a^{k-1}b^{2}
+\cdots
+{}^{k+1}C_{k}ab^{k}
+{}^{k+1}C_{k+1}b^{k+1}.
\end{aligned}
$$
